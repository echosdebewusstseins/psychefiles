THE MECHANISM

Your body isn't broken. It's running on the wrong fuel.

Cortisol is supposed to be emergency fuel — a generator that kicks on during a real threat, then powers back down. If your nervous system learned, early and often, to expect danger around every corner, that generator never gets the signal to shut off.

So when an actual calm moment shows up, your body reads the drop in cortisol the same way it would read a sudden threat. Not because anything is wrong — because something changed, and to a body wired this tightly, change means danger until proven otherwise.

This is the exact mechanism Addicted to Chaos spends 51 pages taking apart — page by page, symptom by symptom — until it stops looking like a character flaw and starts looking like chemistry you can actually work with.
